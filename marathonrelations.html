Also, we have a serious love for Stars and Stripes! ~MRica~" -Sydney Hall, Director of Marathon Relations

High School Marathons

IUDM is honored to work in partnership with over 20 high schools in the state in helping to facilitate their ‘mini’ dance marathons.

Similar to IUDM, high school students raise money for Riley Hospital for Children, learn the IU Mini Linedance, and meet Riley families from the community, among other activities during the marathon!

If you would like any information on how IUDM can assist at your high school’s dance marathon or if you have any questions on how to get started, please contact Sydney Hall, Director of Marathon Relations, at marathon.relations@iudm.org.

We are proud to announce the continued success of all of our high school partners, as well as welcome several new high school dance marathons to our family:

January 19, 2014: Bishop Chatard – Our first marathon of the year Bishop Chatard raised $2,825.00 after taking a break from having a marathon last year. Dating back to 2000 Bishop Chatard Riley Dance Marathon is one of the oldest high school dance marathons. Chatard also created their own 5 minute line dance this year!

January 24, 2014: Castle High School – IUDM traveled to Evansville for Castle’s fourth dance marathon where they raised $11,083.15! This marathon, one of the more unique high school marathons, was planned around the Homecoming basketball game and the students performed the line dance at half time.

February 1, 2014: Center Grove High School – Center Grove Dance Marathon raised $3,072.00 surpassing their total from 2013 by more than $1,000! A fairly new marathon, Center Grove is working to increase DM involvement in their school and is hoping to grow rapidly in the upcoming years.

February 22, 2014: Carmel High School – Continuing to set the record for the nation’s largest high school dance marathon, Carmel High School raised $324,597.54 this year surpassing their 2013 total by $30,000! This year marked Carmel’s ninth dance marathon. Dedicated to Ashley Louise Crouse, Carmel Dance Marathon has brought the story of ALC to thousands of high schoolers as they continue to dance in her honor.

March 8, 2014: Gibson Southern High School – Located in southern Indiana, this marathon in only its second year raised $2,974.82! One of the highlights of this marathon were the local Riley kids who spoke and stole the hearts of everyone in attendance.

March 14, 2014: Whiteland High School – In only their second year of dance marathon, Whiteland raised $12,395.42, surpassing their goal and their 2013 total by almost $5,000. The eagerness and involvement of the students at Whiteland shows for a promising future for their upcoming dance marathons!

March 14, 2014: Plainfield High School – Occurring on the same night as Whiteland Dance Marathon, IUDM had a great time kicking off spring break by attending these two new and thriving marathons. Plainfield raised $6,771.81 this year. Highlights of the marathon included a basketball tournament, zumba, and almost 400 student participants!

March 22, 2014: Cardinal Ritter – A marathon in only its third year, Cardinal Ritter raised $2,046.34 this year. This surpassed their 2013 total by more than $500!

April 11, 2014: New Palestine – Raising $1,829.39 New Palestine almost tripled their 2013 total and surpassed their goal! IUDM had a great time playing in a dodge ball tournament against the New Pal students.

April 12, 2014: Tell City High School – Traveling to southern Indiana IUDM had a great time attending this second year marathon hosted by the schools Key Club. Tell City raised $790.04 and is excited about continuing on dance marathon in years to come.

April 12, 2014: Bloomington High School South/Bloomington High School North – Another one of the largest high school dance marathons in the nation, Bloomington South did an incredible job this year and surpassed $100,000, a goal they’ve been looking to reach for years! Totaling $100,283.14 South passed the 2013 total by $30,000. This marathon also marked a milestone for IUDM, the total raised that night meant IUDM had surpassed the 2013 IUDM high school marathon total.

April 18, 2014: Southport High School/Perry Meridian High School – IUDM’s longest continuous high school dance marathon, dating back to 2000, Southport and Perry Meridian combined raised $23,038.95 this year. This beat the 2013 total by almost $7,000!

April 18, 2014: Carpe Diem School – A first year marathon, Carpe Diem raised a total of $1,417.75, easily surpassing their goal of $1,000! Carpe Diem, a public charter school located in Indianapolis, showed great enthusiasm towards dance marathon and is looking forward to continuing to develop the event for years to come.

April 19, 2014: Hamilton Southeastern High School – Hamilton Southeastern located in Fishers raised $12,508.84 this year surpassing their 2013 total by almost $4,000! Highlights of the marathon included a photo booth and a donut-eating contest. Working to restructure their marathon HSE is excited for increased participation in the future of their marathon. This marathon marked a milestone for IUDM, with HSE’s total IUDM surpassed half a million dollars for the 2014 IUDM high school marathon total.

April 27, 2014: Roncalli High School – With a proud dance marathon tradition Roncalli partnered with South Deanery raised $21,181.96 this year! This highly attended and high-energy marathon lasted 9 hours, and had many activities including an area where students could pay $1 to take a five-minute sitting break.

May 17, 2014: Edgewood High School – In their first year Edgewood high school raised $3,233.97 for the kids! Rounding out IUDM’s spring high school marathons, this marathon had some exciting mat ball and dodge ball competitions. Surpassing their first year goal, Edgewood is excited to get dance marathon rolling and continue working for the kids!

September 27, 2014: Culver Academies – We can’t wait to see!
